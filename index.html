Marilou McFarlane has been a successful leader in the sports tech business for 10 years, working as CEO, founder or as head of business development for innovative startups. Marilou most recently has co-founded the non-profit Women in Sports Tech to drive growth opportunities for women in the business.

As CEO of venture-backed Edufii, the mobile collaboration platform for coaches and athletes, she led the business to significant new funding, pivoted business model to subscription revenue, and grew customers 500% globally in over 60 sports, culminating in successful merger with Shotzoom, the leader in sports and active learning software solutions.

Prior to Edufii, she led business development with iconic organizations such as IMG Academy, US Soccer, Perfect Game Baseball, NBA Scouting and 350+ NCAA programs to increase subscription revenue over 400% for SportsBoard’s mobile player assessment SaaS solution.

She also founded the first digital media property for young female athletes, Vivo Girls Sports, with the mission to keep them playing sports, landing in the top 1% of all Facebook groups globally and inspiring the creation of espnW to cover and support women in sports.

Before discovering her passion for sports tech, Marilou generated over $50M in revenue as a leader in media and sponsorship sales for CBS and iHeartMedia, and providing marketing consulting services for an array of clients in the healthcare (Stanford Health) and tech (Adobe, Autodesk) sectors.

Inspirational coach and teammate, embracing diversity initiatives to drive innovation with a competitor’s spirit, as a 3:06 marathon runner and former UNC varsity athlete. Happy wife of a fire captain, mother to 2 illustrious daughters, 10 year youth soccer coach and Mill Valley Soccer Club president, trail runner and dog lover.
